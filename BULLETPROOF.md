## Phase 2: UPGRADE the Auto Research Engine

This is the most important phase. The auto loop + research engine
must be able to solve problems like:

- "Research every approach to treating pancreatic cancer and find
   novel combinations that haven't been tried"
- "Analyze every market crash since 1900 and find the common
   structural patterns that precede them"
- "Design a spacecraft propulsion system using only materials
   available on Mars"

### Upgrade research.py:

- The research prompts must be MUCH deeper. Not "what are recent findings"
  but "what are the fundamental mechanisms", "what are the competing
  theories", "what evidence supports each theory", "what are the
  mathematical models", "what are the experimental results"

- Add ITERATIVE DEEPENING. First pass = broad survey. Second pass =
  deep dive on most promising areas. Third pass = connecting insights
  across areas. Fourth pass = generating novel hypotheses.

- Add ADVERSARIAL RESEARCH. For every finding, search for the
  counter-argument. "This drug shows promise" → "What are the
  failure modes? What does the opposition say? What studies
  contradict this?"

- Add QUANTITATIVE EXTRACTION. Don't just store "drug X works."
  Store "drug X showed 40% response rate in phase II trials with
  n=200, p<0.01, but only in BRCA-mutated populations."

- Add SOURCE QUALITY SCORING. A Nature paper > a blog post.
  A meta-analysis > a single study. Weight findings by source quality.

- Add TEMPORAL AWARENESS. Recent findings > old findings.
  Track when research was published. Flag outdated information.

### Upgrade synthesis.py:

- The synthesis engine must find NON-OBVIOUS connections.
  Not "A and B are both about cancer." That's trivial.
  "A's mechanism of action on pathway X could explain B's
  unexpected efficacy when combined with compound Y" — THAT'S
  a real synthesis.

- Add HYPOTHESIS TESTING. When synthesis generates a hypothesis,
  immediately research whether it's been tested. If yes, what
  were the results? If no, that's a genuine novel insight.

- Add CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION. Not all syntheses are equal.
  A synthesis supported by 5 independent findings > one
  supported by a single blog post.

- Add CONTRADICTION DETECTION. When two findings or syntheses
  contradict each other, flag it. Contradictions are where
  breakthroughs hide.

### Upgrade auto.py:

- The system prompt must produce a CTO who actually THINKS,
  not just executes. Research → hypothesize → test → revise.

- Add REFLECTION. Every 10 turns, Claude should step back:
  "What have I learned? What's my current understanding?
  What am I still confused about? What should I investigate next?"

- Add MILESTONE TRACKING. "I've completed 3/7 major steps.
  Here's what each step produced. Here's what's remaining."

- Add QUALITY GATES. Before moving to the next step, verify
  the current step is actually correct. Not "it compiled" but
  "it solves the actual problem correctly."

- Add CONTEXT RECOVERY. When context compacts, don't just
  re-inject the goal. Re-inject the entire state: what's been
  done, what's been learned, what decisions were made and why.

## Phase 3: UPGRADE the Knowledge Graph

- Entity extraction must be MUCH smarter. Not just regex patterns.
  Detect: people, organizations, papers, methods, chemicals,
  genes, proteins, mathematical concepts, code patterns.

- Add RELATIONSHIP TYPES. Not just "entity A co-occurs with entity B."
  "A causes B", "A contradicts B", "A is a component of B",
  "A supersedes B", "A is analogous to B in domain C."

- Add CONFIDENCE DECAY. Old knowledge loses confidence over time
  unless reinforced. A fact from 6 months ago that hasn't been
  referenced is less reliable than one from yesterday.

- Add KNOWLEDGE VALIDATION. Periodically verify stored facts are
  still current. Research has retractions. Code changes. Decisions
  get revised.
